In this assignment, you'll analyze a collection of data sets from the San Francisco Open Data Portal and Zillow. The data sets have been stored in the SQLite database sf_data.sqlite, which you can download here. The database contains the following tables:

Table Description
crime Crime reports dating back to 2010.
mobile_food_locations List of all locations where mobile food vendors sell.
mobile_food_permits List of all mobile food vendor permits. More details here.
mobile_food_schedule Schedules for mobile food vendors.
noise Noise complaints dating back to August 2015.
parking List of all parking lots.
parks List of all parks.
schools List of all schools.
zillow Zillow rent and housing statistics dating back to 1996. More details here.

The mobile_food_ tables are explicitly connected through the locationid and permit columns. The other tables are not connected, but you may be able to connect them using dates, latitude/longitude, or postal codes.

Shapefiles for US postal codes are available here. These may be useful for converting latitude/longitude to postal codes.

Shapefiles for San Francisco Neighborhoods are available here.

Analysis¶

From the plots above we see that there seems to be an upward trend regarding Parks and Schools. There appears to be a large congregation of schools and parks in the middle of the city. There appears to be more parks than schools next to the Marina and Fisherman's Wharf. This could be due to those areas being more touristy and not as residential as other parts of San Francisco. The largest amount of schools in a zip code appear to be Pacific Heights, an affluent,residential district in San Francisco where there might be more families. The mission district also contains a large amount of schools. Both of these areas also have a small amount of parks per school. Areas which do not include many schools are the Port and Golden Gate Park Districts, both of these areas also contain a small amount of parks. The Port and Golden Gate Park both have smaller residential populations than the other districts because one's a park and the other is a commercial hub. Thus we fail to conclude a significant relationship between parks and schools because areas with more tourist traffic will have more parks and less residents, thus skewing the data.

Analysis¶

From the above plot we see that most of the food trucks are actually near the eastern part of San Francisco. It seems taht food trucks are less concerned with serving people in residential areas and at parks and more interested in selling at areas where there is large commerical interest. It's is also surpising to see the lack of food trucks in Fisherman's Wharf and the Marina where there is a lot of Tourist activity. From what this plot shows, the Financial district seems to be the primary area where food trucks are centered. This suggests that many people who go on lunch breaks throughout the day or get off work will visit food trucks. We also see from the public transit lines plot that many of the food trucks follow public transportation lines while parks will further extend west. While most of the Food Trucks are located in the financial district, the ones outside the commercial district do tend to be located near the parks.
